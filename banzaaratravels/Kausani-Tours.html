Bageshwar

A small city bearing huge religious significance, Bageshwar it is adorned with an array of Hindu temples like Panchnama Junnarka, Dattatreya Maharaj Temple, Hanuman Temple, Bhairav Temple, Kalika Temple, and of course Bageshwar Mahadev. Thanks to its heart throbbing beauty, Bageshwar is also considered as an important tourist destination. This serene combo of culture and natural beauty makes Bageshwar a significant point of interest for the tourists and devotees alike. Legend has it that Lord Shiva visited the Bageshwar Mahadev temple in the form of a tiger when Sage Markandeya lived here. Therefore, this Shiva temple is specifically popular and has been named after this divine incident.

In the event that you need to trade the city life for some bird tune, Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary is the best escape to be with the birds and untamed life. Binsar Sanctuary was set up in 1988, and spreads over a space of 45.59 sq kms. Initially, set up for the preservation and security of the contracting wide leaf Oak (Quercus) backwoods of the Central Himalayan district, it is now home to an assortment of verdure, and more than 200 types of birds including Forktail, Blackbirds, Laughing Thrush, Kalij Pheasant, Nuthatches, Parakeets and Monal.

A mainstream cascade situated on Kausani-Almora Road, it's anything but a delightful regular waterfall, whose water tumbles from a stature and streams with the waterway. There are numerous old caverns here, which have been found close to the cascade. As indicated by legends, this spot is associated with Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu and hence, was named after them. Someshwar

A less known but adoring town situated on the banks of Kosi and Sai rivers is Someshwar - a village with its own charm and heavenly beauty. The valley of Someshwar is surrounded by peaks known as Jayanti and Airi. with their names respectively based on two Hindu Goddesses. The village of Someshwar itself is a holy place dedicated to Lord Shiva. Ecotourism is present in the village but not at the scale it should be, as Someshwar still remains to be a hidden gem of Uttarakhand. The Someshwar valley is divided into two sub-vallies - Lod Valley and Boraro Valley. Both the valleys are also called the Bowl of Rice.
